Tonkotsu Ramen

Description

Tonkotsu ramen is a hearty pork-based soup that has become one of the most popular varieties of Japanese ramen.

Ingredients

  • For the soup base:
    • 1 pound split pig's trotters
    • 2 green onions
    • ½ yellow onion, unpeeled, quartered
    • 1 1-inch piece ginger, unpeeled

  • To assemble:
    • Sea salt, to taste
    • Soy sauce, to taste
    • 7 ounces fresh ramen noodles
    • 4 slices chashu pork (pork belly or loin cooked in soy sauce and mirin)
    • ¼ cup enoki mushrooms
    • 2 pieces menma (fermented bamboo shoots)
    • 2 pieces nori
    • 1 shoyu tamago (soy sauce egg) or soft boiled egg, halved
    • ¼ cup green onions, thinly sliced
    • 1 tablespoon coarsely ground sesame seeds
    • Mayu (black garlic oil) or chili oil, to taste 1

Steps

  1. Make the pork broth. Transfer the trotters to a large stock pot and cover with cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, then remove from the heat and drain in a colander, discarding the liquid.
  2. Using chopsticks, clean the bones under cold running water to remove any red or brown blood or organ pieces.
  3. Transfer the cleaned bones to a clean stock pot.
  4. In a large cast iron skillet over medium-high heat, toast the green onions, yellow onion, and ginger, turning occasionally, until the aromatics are charred in places.
  5. Add charred aromatics to the stock pot with the cleaned bones and cover with cold water.
  6. Bring to a boil over high heat, skimming off any scum that floats to the surface. Continue to boil and skim for 20 minutes, then cover and reduce to a simmer.
  7. Continue simmering, adding enough water as needed to keep bones and aromatics covered, until broth is opaque, about 6 hours.
  8. Uncover and bring to a boil. Let broth reduce to desired thickness.
  9. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh strainer and refrigerate overnight.
  10. When cool, bone broth should be very thick and gelatinous, and there should be a layer of fat on top of the broth.
  11. In a stock pot, bring pork bone broth and pork fat to a simmer over medium heat.
  12. Season to taste with salt and soy sauce.
  13. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of water to a boil.
  14. Cook ramen noodles in boiling water according to package directions.
  15. Divide the ramen noodles between two bowls and ladle the pork broth over the noodles.
  16. Top each bowl with two slices of chashu pork, enoki mushrooms, and menma.
  17. Tuck a nori sheet in between the side of the bowl and an egg half.
  18. Sprinkle the bowls with green onions and sesame seeds, and drizzle with mayu and/or chili oil, as desired.
